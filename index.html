01   Executive Summary

Opptra's BD operation is at an inflection point. A 3-person team is managing 100+ active leads across disconnected tools — Notion, Gmail, Slack, and legacy Google Sheets — with no unified pipeline, no stage-level SLAs, and no automated hand-offs. The result: leads fall through cracks, verbal commitments go untracked, ownership is ambiguous, and rejected deals disappear from view entirely.

The Proposal: Build the Opptra BD Command Center — a Notion-native CRM with Claude AI as the orchestration brain, integrated with Gmail and Slack. Standardized stages, enforced SLAs, clear ownership, and AI-powered automation — deployed in a 1-week sprint.

Expected outcomes: near-zero lead leakage, 70% reduction in manual data entry, full stage-transition visibility, automated stakeholder notifications, and data-driven pipeline forecasting — without adding headcount or switching to enterprise CRM software.

Why this approach over Salesforce or HubSpot?  For a 3-person team with an existing Notion ecosystem, a Notion + Claude solution deploys in days (not months), costs a fraction, and provides AI-native automation that legacy CRMs require expensive add-ons to match. Details in Section 08.

03   Proposed Solution: Opptra BD Command Center

The BD Command Center is not a new tool purchase — it is an intelligent orchestration layer built on top of tools your team already uses. Notion becomes the single source of truth. Claude AI becomes the always-on operations manager. Gmail and Slack become automatic input feeds.

3.2   Four Pillars of the Solution

① Unified Stage Pipeline

7 clearly defined stages with entry/exit criteria, maximum duration SLAs, and automated transitions. Plus dedicated Rejected and On-Hold tracks with reason codes and revival workflows.

② Smart Ownership Matrix

Every deal has exactly one primary owner at all times. Ownership auto-transfers at defined stage transitions. Support tags for central team dependencies. BD team retains governance control.

③ AI Automation Layer

Claude monitors Gmail and Slack in real-time, auto-creates leads, captures commitments, enforces SLAs, calculates revenue potential, flags red-flag deals, and broadcasts updates to stakeholders.

④ Governance & Escalation

BD team as CRM custodians with admin privileges. Stage transitions require owner confirmation. Full audit trail on every change. Automated escalation when SLAs breach or red flags trigger.

06   1-Week Implementation Roadmap

Sprint Philosophy: Ship the 80% that matters. Build the core pipeline + ownership model + top-3 automations in Week 1. Layer on advanced AI features iteratively in Weeks 2-4.
Day 1 (Mon) — Foundation

Goal: Notion CRM database schema live and validated.

  • Create Deals database with all fields (stage, owner, SLA dates, revenue, red flags, etc.)
  • Create Brands database with relational link to Deals
  • Create Activity Log database
  • Set up Notion formulas: Days-in-Stage, SLA Status (On Track / Warning / Breached)
  • Build pipeline views: Board view (by stage), Table view (all deals), Filtered views (my deals, SLA warnings)

⚠ Risk: Field definitions may need iteration — keep schema flexible.

Day 2 (Tue) — Stage Alignment & Data Migration

Goal: Team aligned on stage definitions; existing data migrated.

  • Workshop with BD + Brand team: review and finalize stage definitions (30-60 min)
  • Finalize SLA durations per stage
  • Assign ownership of all current 100+ deals
  • Migrate data from Google Sheets → Notion (manual import or CSV)
  • Classify existing deals into correct stages
  • Tag rejected/on-hold deals with reason codes

⚠ Risk: Data quality from GSheets may require cleanup. Budget 2 extra hours.

Day 3 (Wed) — Ownership Model & Governance

Goal: RACI finalized; governance rules configured in Notion.

  • Finalize RACI matrix with team
  • Configure Notion permissions: BD Admin vs. BD User vs. Brand Team vs. View-only
  • Set up ownership transfer protocol (stage-triggered)
  • Create Notion templates for each stage transition (pre-filled checklists)
  • Document SOP for deal owners: what to update, when, how
Day 4 (Thu) — Integration Setup

Goal: Gmail and Slack connected to Notion via Claude.

  • Set up Gmail → Notion pipeline (Claude monitors inbox, parses brand emails, creates/updates deals)
  • Set up Slack → Notion pipeline (Claude monitors BD channels, captures commitments)
  • Configure Claude automation rules (lead detection patterns, commitment extraction prompts)
  • Set up outbound notifications: Notion → Slack (stage changes, SLA alerts)
  • Test with 5-10 sample emails and Slack messages

⚠ Risk: Integration reliability — build manual override for Day 1 issues.

Day 5 (Fri) — AI Automations

Goal: Core automations live — SLA monitor, notifications, red flag detector.

  • Deploy SLA monitoring automation (daily check + real-time triggers)
  • Deploy stakeholder notification automation (stage transitions)
  • Deploy red flag detection (basic rule set: distributor conflicts, margin thresholds)
  • Deploy duplicate detection
  • Configure revenue potential estimation (basic model for MQL+ deals)
  • End-to-end testing of full workflow: email in → deal created → stage advanced → notification sent
Day 6 (Sat/Mon) — Training & UAT

Goal: Team trained and confident; bugs caught before go-live.

  • 30-minute training session: how to use the CRM, what automations do, what to do manually
  • Each team member walks through 3 deals end-to-end
  • Collect feedback on fields, views, and automation behavior
  • Fix critical issues surfaced during UAT
  • Prepare "cheat sheet" reference card for quick lookups
Day 7 — Go-Live & Monitoring

Goal: System is live. Team uses it as the primary deal management tool.

  • Official go-live: all deals managed through Notion CRM from today
  • Monitor Claude automation accuracy for first 24 hours
  • Dedicated Slack channel for system issues / feedback
  • Daily check-in for first week post-launch
  • Plan Week 2-4 enhancements based on real usage data

8.2   Why Notion + Claude Wins

vs. Salesforce

Salesforce is the gold standard at scale — but for a 3-person team, it's a sledgehammer for a nail. Implementation takes months, requires a dedicated admin, and the AI features (Einstein) cost extra. Your team would spend more time configuring Salesforce than closing deals. Revisit if team grows to 15+ people.

vs. HubSpot

HubSpot's free tier is compelling but limited in workflow automation. The features you need (custom SLAs, AI parsing, Slack integration) sit behind paid tiers ($800+/mo). More importantly, your team already lives in Notion — adding HubSpot means maintaining two systems. The AI capabilities are a generation behind Claude.

vs. Status Quo

The status quo is actively losing deals and burning team time. With 33+ leads per person and no automation, your team is spending more time on admin than selling. Every week of delay is another set of leads leaking out of the pipeline.

Scale-Up Path

Notion + Claude is not a dead end. If Opptra scales to 500+ leads or 15+ BD reps, you can migrate to Salesforce/HubSpot with clean data and proven processes. Building discipline in Notion first means you'll actually use the enterprise CRM properly later.

09   Risks & Mitigation

R1: Team Adoption Resistance Medium Likelihood High Impact

Team members may resist changing habits, especially if the new system feels like more work initially.

Mitigation: Design the system to reduce work, not add it. Automations must demonstrably save time from Day 1. Run 30-min training; assign "CRM champion" on the team. First-week daily check-ins to resolve friction immediately.

R2: Data Quality from Migration High Likelihood Medium Impact

Existing GSheets data may be incomplete, inconsistent, or outdated. Migrating garbage creates a garbage CRM.

Mitigation: Day 2 includes a data cleanup pass. Only migrate active deals; archive stale ones. Accept that some deals will need manual re-classification in Week 1.

R3: Integration Reliability Medium Likelihood Medium Impact

Gmail/Slack → Notion automations may misfire, miss messages, or create incorrect records.

Mitigation: Build a "human-in-the-loop" model for Week 1: Claude proposes changes, team confirms critical ones. Set confidence thresholds — only auto-create deals above 80% confidence. Daily accuracy review for first 2 weeks.

R4: Claude AI Accuracy Low Likelihood Medium Impact

Claude may misclassify emails, miss commitments, or generate incorrect revenue estimates.

Mitigation: Start with conservative automation — auto-suggest rather than auto-execute for the first 2 weeks. Refine prompts and rules based on actual performance. Revenue estimates should always show as ranges with confidence levels, not single numbers.

R5: 1-Week Timeline Pressure Medium Likelihood Low Impact

Ambitious timeline may result in cutting corners or launching with bugs.

Mitigation: Prioritize ruthlessly. Core CRM + stage definitions + ownership model are non-negotiable for Week 1. AI automations can launch in "suggest mode" first and auto-execute in Week 2-3. Better to ship 80% that works than 100% that doesn't.

R6: Over-Automation Low Likelihood Medium Impact

Too many notifications and auto-actions could create noise fatigue, causing team to ignore the system.

Mitigation: Start with minimal notifications (critical only). Let the team request more rather than opt-out of less. Weekly review of notification volume and relevance.
